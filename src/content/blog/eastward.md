## Once in a while you get to play a game that just "clicks" with who you are and what you've gone and your current situation.

For me it was *Eastward*, developed by *Pixpil* and published by *Chucklefish*, released in September 2021 (by the end of the lockdowns in Chile), it had caught my eye just a few months before launch with its gorgeous pixel art and music so it was a release date buy for me.

[scene[0]]

## The game starts with a beautiful short animated intro and a forboding main menu.

After picking the start button one of my favorite story telling tropes takes places "the story within the story" in this case a videogame that as the game progresses mirrors our protagonist's journey.

## From the very beggining the selling points of the game meet their promise.

Colorful enviorments that feel alive and cute memorable characters, even though detailed pixel art comes with an inherit visual stacticity given the effort it takes both
