## Once in a while you get to play a game that just "clicks" with who you are and what you've gone and your current situation.

For me it was *Eastward*, developed by *Pixpil* and published by *Chucklefish*, released in September 2021 (by the end of the lockdowns in Chile), it had caught my eye just a few months before launch with its gorgeous pixel art and music so it was a release date buy for me. The game starts with a beautiful short animated intro and a forboding main menu.

[scene[0]]

After picking the start button one of my favorite story telling tropes takes places "the story within the story" in this case a videogame that as the game progresses mirrors our protagonist's journey.
From the very beggining the selling points of the game meet their promise. Colorful enviorments that feel alive and cute memorable characters, even though detailed pixel art comes with an inherit visual stacticity given the effort it takes both. Memorable characters, locations and set-pieces are what have stayed the most after a few years.

## Simple and Polished

The gameloop, consists in exploration (talk/roam), a small harmless activity, exploration and a level/dungeon, with puzzles and a plethora of enemies and bosses. The game's main mechanic is the control of our protagonists, the ever happy and cheerful *Sam* and *John* our silent gruffy father figure(GREAT GEORGE MICHAEL SONG).
